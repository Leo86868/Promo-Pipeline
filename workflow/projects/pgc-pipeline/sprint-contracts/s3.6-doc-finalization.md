### Mermaid skip-or-include decision (for AC18)

Per-doc matrix is locked in the Visual quality section above. The judgment rules behind the matrix:

**A diagram is earning its keep if:**
1. It conveys **branching logic, parallel paths, or feedback loops** that are hard to convey linearly in prose.
2. Every **edge has a labeled relationship** (not just an arrow). "Used by" is too vague — say WHAT (e.g., "retry helper", "MiMo prompt", "on ClipAssignmentError").
3. The diagram is readable in **one glance** — typically ≤8 nodes, ≤12 edges. If you need >2 sentences of caption to explain it, simplify or split.
4. It surfaces an **invariant or constraint** (e.g., `FreezeWouldOccurError` raised here) at the right node — turns the diagram into a debugging aid.

**A diagram is decoration (skip) if:**
1. It just shows a linear A → B → C sequence prose covers in 1 sentence.
2. Edges are unlabeled or all say "calls" / "uses".
3. The diagram needs the prose to make sense — meaning the prose was already enough.
4. The doc's content is a flat inventory of files (e.g., `cli/`'s 6 standalone scripts) — a table conveys the information without the visual overhead.

**Mid-build add-without-approval is forbidden for the 4 skip docs** (`analyze/`, `llm/`, `cli/`, plus `selection/` whose default is skip). If the builder thinks one of these docs would benefit from a diagram during the rewrite, **stop and ask the operator** before adding it. Default is to skip per the matrix.
